Angkor Wat, Campuchia

The world's largest religious monument Angkor Wat is part of a vast temple complex in northwestern Cambodia, 12 large temples and many smaller ones. With the pinnacle works of Khmer architectural style, Angkor Wat has become a symbol of Cambodia, appearing on the national flag and is the country's top tourist attraction.

Taj Mahal, India

The Taj Mahal is a mausoleum built in the 1600s, inlaid with ivory white marble. The grounds feature aristocratic gardens, beautiful walkways, flowerbeds and iconic reflecting pools. With a harmonious beauty, the Taj Mahal complex is known as a monument for eternal love.

Machu Picchu, Peru

The Inca city of Machu Picchu was located on a flattened peak of the Andes. This prehistoric wonder is said to be the site of the study of astronomical elements. The site is famous for its breathtaking views and sparkling granite wall ruins built without any mortar.

Pyramid Giza, Egypt

The list of the most famous historical monuments in the world would not be complete without mentioning the pyramids of Giza. The complex has 6 large pyramids, several cemeteries, settlements and a palace complex that includes large Sphinxes. The pharaohs believed that they would become gods in the afterlife. So they stored a lot of wealth in the pyramids. However, even if these pyramids have lost their treasures, this place still attracts visitors with its massive and magnificent beauty.

Chichen Itza, Mexico

Chichén Itzá was one of the most important Maya cities in the Yucatan peninsula of Mexico, flourishing from 400 until the 1400s. Due to the wide variety of Mesoamerican (Pre-Columbian) architectural styles found, It is said to have the most diverse population in the Maya world. The Great Ball Court, the Temple of the Warriors, and El Castillo are some of the most striking structures here.

Moai Statue on Easter Island Chile

A volcanic island with undulating hills and lush palm forests, Easter Island is famous for its hundreds of monolithic statues known as Moai. The prehistoric people of the Rapa Nui civilization carved these massive statues hundreds of years ago in memory of their ancestors. Because the Moai statues are made of stone blocks nearly 4 meters high and weigh 14 tons, no one knows how they were moved to the island.


Stonehenge, Anh

This megalithic monument is located in the heart of a Neolithic and Bronze Age site near Amesbury, England. Stonehenge consists of a circle of giant standing stones, is one of the most mysterious historical sites in the world, as archaeologists are still trying to figure out the original purpose of the work. It is theorized that this place was used to study the movement of the Sun and Moon or used for religious purposes.

The Colosseum, Italy

The Colosseum is one of the largest theaters in the world with a capacity of more than 50,000 people. Tourists from all over the world come here to admire the remains of a civilization that once ruled the world. Almost two-thirds of the building has been destroyed, however, this magnificent structure remains one of the most famous and worth visiting Roman landmarks.

Great Wall,China

As China's greatest man-made wonder, the Great Wall stretches for tens of thousands of kilometers through hills and forests. This spectacular structure took more than 500 years to build, with the main purpose of defense, against the invasion of the Mongols.
